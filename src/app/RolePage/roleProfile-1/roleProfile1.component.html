  • Vladimir Putin

  • Biography:
    Vladimir Vladimirovich Putin was born on October 7th, 1952 in Leningrad in the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics A.K.A USSR (now St. Petersburg in the Russian Federation). Putin is the youngest of three children by Vladimir Spiridonovich Putin (1911–1999) and Maria Ivanovna Putina (1911–1998).

    From ages 7-15 (1960-1968), Vladimir Putin attended Primary School No. 193 in Leningrad. After 1968 he attended Leningrad High School No. 281. A school that was a “chemistry- focused magnet school”. Putin completed his studies at High School No. 281 1970 at the age of 17.

    In the 1970’s Putin entered higher tertiary education. Beginning his higher education as a law student at Leningrad State University, finishing his degree in 1975. Sometime in the late 1970’s-early 1980’s Putin studied at the KGB (Soviet Union’s main intelligence agency) School No. 1 (A.K.A the Andropov Institute) in Moscow. He then worked as a foreign intelligence officer for 15 years. Before returning to Russia to retire from active KGB service with the rank of lieutenant colonel. During this time, he had met and married Lyudmila Shkrebneva. Additionally, producing two daughters, Maria (1985) and Katerina (1986).

    After returning to Leningrad in 1990 Putin found a job as assistant (prorector) to the rector of Leningrad State University. Putin then became an advisor to Anatoly Sobchak, the first mayor of St. Petersburg to be democratically elected. By 1994 Putin held the post of first deputy mayor. By 1996 Putin had moved himself and his family to Moscow to join presidential staff as deputy to Pavel Borodin, the Kremlin’s chief administrator. From this point on Putin’s political career rose rapidly. March 1997, Putin is appointed Deputy Chief of Staff of the Presidential Executive Office and Chief of Main Control Directorate. July 1998, Putin is appointed Director of the Federal Security Service by Russian President, Boris Yeltsin (the domestic branch of the KGB’s successor). Shortly after becoming Secretary of the Security Council of the Russian Federation. Then in August 1999, Putin is offered the job of Prime Minister of Russia by the then Russian President Boris Yeltsin, which Putin accepts. December 31 1999, Boris Yeltsin steps down as President, making Putin the acting president.

    Putin stayed as acting President until the official elections were held in March 2000. He won this election and continued his role as President. In the 2004 elections Putin was once again re-elected. However, due to term limits within the Russian constitution he was prevented re- election in 2008. Instead Dmitry Medvedev ran for president. Upon winning the election Medvedev appointed Putin as Russia's prime minister. In the 2012 Russian election, Putin was once again re-elected to perform a third-term as President.
